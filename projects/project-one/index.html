V-Shield

Posted: 0001-01-01

A Byzantine-Resilient V-Formation Demonstrator

Introduction

V-Shield is a Python/Matplotlib/NetworkX demo of a small UAV swarm flying in V-formation while under attack by “Byzantine” agents. Inspired by the recent work “Resilience for Distributed Consensus with Constraints” by X. Wang, S. Mou & S. Sundaram, we solve a LP to compute a (γ, α)-resilient convex combination of its neighbors’ positions, automatically ignoring up to κ malicious inputs, yet still guarantees that the flock converges rapidly to and maintains the desired V-shape.

Key ingredients

Robust network design We pick a communication graph satisfying the paper’s r-robustness conditions (enough disjoint in-neighbor redundancy) so that even if each drone loses information from κ corrupt neighbors, the remaining honest links still span a rooted tree.

Resilient convex combinations At each time-step each honest drone

Gathers its σ = n κ + 2 candidate next-step “virtual” positions (its own plus its σ–1 nearest neighbors).

Builds all C(σ–1, σ–κ–1) subsets of size σ–κ, and solves a small LP that finds one point φ in the intersection of their convex hulls (plus its own convex hull).

Uses φ as its new formation-offset, thereby excluding any influence from up to κ malicious drones.

Exponential convergence

By the paper’s theorems (and its constraint-redundancy corollary), this LP-filtering update provably drives the honest drones’ positions to exact consensus on the V-shape at an exponential rate, even though the Byzantines may broadcast arbitrary positions.

Visualization

We animate the swarm flying over a grass-green background, drawing each drone as a PNG icon, plus a little top-left info box showing the corrupt agents’ true positions.

Takeaway

With only local information and a quick LP at each step, no global knowledge of the network or which agents are bad, V-Shield implements the core ideas of the paper and demonstrates a practical, efficient way to safeguard consensus-based formation control against the most powerful (Byzantine) attacks.
